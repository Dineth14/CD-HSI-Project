About the Project

Change detection is a key remote sensing task that aims to identify and interpret variations on the Earth’s surface by analyzing images acquired at different times. These variations may result from natural events such as floods, wildfires, landslides, and seasonal changes, or from human-driven activities such as urbanization, deforestation, construction, and agricultural expansion. Accurate change detection provides essential information for environmental monitoring, disaster assessment, land-use planning, and sustainable resource management. Recent advances in computer vision and deep learning have significantly improved the ability to extract reliable change information from bi-temporal remote sensing imagery, making change detection an important research direction for understanding and monitoring dynamic geographical transformations.

Our project focuses on building a change detection algorithm, reviewing the limitations of modern techniques, and proposing improvements for more accurate and efficient detection.
