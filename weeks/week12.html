Week 12 — Training on Benchmark Change Detection Datasets

Status: Completed · Dates: 12/07/2026 – 18/07/2026
← Week 11  ·  Home  ·  Week 13 →

Planned Work

  • Train the proposed change detection architecture on the DSIFN-CD and WHU-CD benchmark datasets.
  • Evaluate model predictions against ground truth using F1 score, mIoU, Overall Accuracy (OA), Recall, Precision, and Boundary F1 (BF1).
  • Compare our model's performance against the top 5 state-of-the-art benchmark models for each dataset.

Completed Work

  • Validated detection quality across complex multi-scale land-cover changes and fine object boundaries.
  • Compiled quantitative comparative analysis against the top 5 performing methods on DSIFN-CD and WHU-CD.

Key Findings

  • Outperformance on DSIFN-CD: Our proposed model achieves 0.7127 F1 and 0.7223 mIoU, surpassing the previous top-performing model (BIT-CD with 0.6732 F1) by +3.95% in F1 score and +31.4% in Boundary F1 (BF1: 0.3705 vs 0.2819).
  • State-of-the-Art on WHU-CD: On the WHU-CD benchmark, our VMamba-base model sets a new benchmark high with 0.9617 F1, 0.9612 mIoU, 0.9963 OA, and 0.9321 BF1, exceeding ChangeMamba (0.9521 F1, 0.8605 BF1).
